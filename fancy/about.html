About Sand & Stars

Canyon country, done right.

Moab-based. Locally guided. Built around a simple idea: your day in the desert is worth more than a seat on a crowded van.

Sand & Stars Touring is a small private tour outfit based in Moab, Utah. We run permitted tours of Arches and Canyonlands National Parks, Dead Horse Point State Park, and the BLM backcountry that stitches them together.

We started for the reason most small tour companies start: we kept watching people spend a week planning a trip to the desert, then burn the best day of it stuck in a line at the park entrance, missing half the things that would have stopped them in their tracks.

Everything we do is aimed at the opposite of that. Private by default. Just your group, one vehicle. Local guides who actually live here and know the light, the weather, the crowd patterns. Itineraries shaped on the day, not scripts read off a clipboard.

"You don't sell a tour. You sell the one good day the whole trip gets remembered for."

We're a real small business. Permits, insurance, the whole stack. Not a clearinghouse sending you to strangers. When you book with us, you're booking us. The owner still drives. The guides you meet are the guides whose names we know.

How We Actually Work

Four things we don't compromise on

01

Private group, always

You'll never share a vehicle with strangers. Every tour is yours. Your pace, your stops, your schedule.

02

Real local guides

Our guides live in Moab year-round. Not weekenders, not gig-hoppers. They know the park calendar inside out.

03

We shape the day around what the desert is doing. Heat, wind, monsoon, low-angle light. That flexibility is the whole point.

Permits & Standards

Fully permitted. Professionally run.

We operate under Commercial Use Authorizations from the National Park Service for Arches and Canyonlands, a permit from Utah State Parks for Dead Horse Point, and Special Recreation Permits from the Bureau of Land Management for the surrounding canyon country.

Our guides carry current Wilderness First Aid, our vehicles are inspected and insured for commercial passenger service, and we follow Leave No Trace practices on every tour. None of this is a marketing line. It's how a legitimate tour company has to operate.

You shouldn't have to ask whether the company driving you into the desert is permitted and insured. With us, the answer is yes, on paper, every time.
