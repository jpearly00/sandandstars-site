Gallery

Canyon country, seen.

A curated photographic record of tours through Arches, Canyonlands, and the wider Moab region.

The Gallery is the photographic memory of every trip we've run. Rather than throw up a random grid of stock-feeling images, we're building it out intentionally — organized, captioned, tied to the stories and the places behind each shot.

Instructional PlaceholderThe Gallery is blocked on the photo library project currently in progress. Rather than launch it with a generic sampling, we're holding it until the curated set is ready. Everything below describes what the finished page will be.
What The Finished Gallery Will Contain

Four sections, one scroll

Section 1 — By Destination. Arches, Canyonlands (Island in the Sky, Needles, Maze overlooks), Dead Horse Point, Moab rock art, and the BLM backcountry that connects them. Each destination gets a short row of signature images — not every photo, just the ones that define the place. A caption under each explains what it is, when it was taken, and what tour it came from.

Section 2 — By Season. Spring wildflowers, summer monsoons, autumn long-light, winter snow on red rock. The seasonal character of the desert is half the reason to come, and the gallery is where that shows up first.

Section 3 — The Human Moments. Guests at overlooks, kids scrambling slickrock, the pause before someone walks out onto the canyon rim for the first time. These are the photos guests share. They belong on the site.

Section 4 — Behind The Scenes. Permits, vehicles, the team, early-morning coffee in the driveway before a sunrise tour, the unglamorous parts of running a real tour company. Honesty shows, and people notice.

Why It's Not Published Yet

The photo library rebuild

We have tens of thousands of photos across a decade of tours. Most of them live in folders on various drives, unsorted and uncaptioned. The project currently in progress is consolidating that archive, selecting the 200 or so photos worth surfacing on the site, tagging them with destination and season and tour type, and preparing optimized web-ready versions at multiple sizes for fast loading on mobile.

Publishing a gallery before this work is done would mean publishing whatever happens to be easy to grab — which is the wrong filter. We'd rather wait and launch the gallery we want.

Tour-specific imagery on the Tours page is already live and will migrate into the gallery once the curated set is ready.

Want To See More Now?

A few places the best photos already live

The Tours page has a photo for each tour type. The Stories page will eventually carry the narrative photography that pairs with guest accounts. Our Google reviews page is also a surprisingly good place to see candid, unfiltered shots from people who've been on our tours.
