Bespoke Expeditions

The trip of a lifetime, designed around you.

Multi-day custom expeditions. Mighty 5, Grand Circle, and everything in between.

The Bespoke page is the centerpiece of the new site. It's where someone planning a trip-of-a-lifetime decides whether we're the outfit to build it with them. We're treating it as a slow page — one we want to shoot for specifically before we publish it — rather than a placeholder we stuff with stock copy.

What The Finished Page Will Contain

Three blocks, each doing a specific job

Block 1 — What "bespoke" actually means here. A plain-language definition of our Bespoke Expeditions tier: multi-day, fully personalized, lodging and logistics handled end to end, Mighty 5 and Grand Circle itineraries included. Differentiated clearly from our half-day and full-day tours, which are already bespoke in the sense that they're private, but sit at a different price and scope.

Block 2 — Three example expeditions as case studies. Drawn from our actual past trips: a two-day Arches + Canyonlands deep-dive, a 5-day Mighty 5 arc, and a 10-day Grand Circle loop. Each with a day-by-day sketch, a few hero photos, and the kind of detail that helps someone picture themselves doing it.

Block 3 — The build process. How we actually design a bespoke trip: initial email, a planning call, draft itinerary with alternates, lodging recommendations by style and budget, final confirmation, a pre-trip document, and a guide who knows your itinerary cold. We've refined this over hundreds of trips. It should be easy to understand and easy to imagine.

Why It's Not Published Yet

Two data projects need to finish first

Photo library. We're rebuilding our photo archive — sorting, tagging, culling, and selecting the hero images that belong on the site. The bespoke page in particular depends on having a handful of knockout multi-day-expedition images that show the scale of what these trips look like: river canyons, overlooks at sunset, alpine-to-desert transitions, the whole arc. Until those images are ready, the visual half of the page doesn't work.

Email analysis. A parallel project is reading through years of guest email threads — the pre-trip exchanges, the "what should we see" conversations, the post-trip thank-you notes — to pull out what guests actually care about, what questions they ask first, which phrases land. That signal will shape the copy here so the page speaks directly to what someone reading it is actually thinking.

Once both projects ship, the bespoke page gets written once, from real material, rather than twice from guesswork.
