Stories

The trips that stuck.

Guest narratives, trip recaps from our side, and longer writing about canyon country.

Stories is the human layer of the site. The narratives that a tour page can't carry and a review snippet can't do justice. Guest accounts, trip recaps, the why-we-do-this pieces, the occasional longer read about a specific place or season.

Instructional PlaceholderThe Stories page is blocked on the guest-email analysis project. Rather than ship generic content or recycle Wix copy, we're waiting for that work to finish so the stories are drawn from real guest voices, with their permission, and told with the detail that makes a narrative actually land.
What The Finished Stories Page Will Contain

Three story types, each doing a different job

Type 1. Guest narratives. A handful of longer guest accounts of their Moab trips, with permission and photos. Not reviews. Actual stories, with beginnings and middles and ends, that capture what a day or a week with us was really like. Written in the guest's voice where possible, with light editing.

Type 2. Trip recaps from our side. Notable tours from our perspective. The multi-day bespoke that went somewhere unexpected, the sunrise that rewrote the itinerary, the guest who turned out to be a rock-art specialist, the rainstorm that made the day. Told in first person by the guide or by John.

Type 3. Place pieces. Longer-form writing about specific places we guide. The archaeology behind a rock-art panel, the geology of Dead Horse Point, why the Needles district feels different from Island in the Sky, what a summer monsoon actually looks like from a canyon overlook. The kind of content a good guidebook would have, if guidebooks still got written carefully.

Why It's Not Published Yet

The guest-email analysis project

Years of guest email threads. Pre-trip planning, post-trip thank-yous, the occasional long letter. Live in our inbox. That archive is being read and distilled into signal: what phrases guests use to describe their days, which stories keep coming back, which trips guests remember years later, which questions they had before the trip and which answers actually landed.

That's the source material for Stories. Without it, the page would be us guessing what resonates. And we'd rather not guess. We'd rather lift the real thing, with permission, and tell it honestly.

Once the analysis is done, we'll reach out to select past guests whose trips merit a longer write-up and ask if they're willing to contribute. Their voice, our photos, light editing. Then it gets published.

In The Meantime

Where the short-form stories already live

Our homepage carries a rotating band of guest reviews pulled live from Google. Those are the short-form version of what Stories will be. The About page has the why-we-do-this piece. Tour-level narrative lives on each tour page.

Have a story from your trip with us you'd be willing to have featured? Email tours@sandandstars.com. We'd be honored.
